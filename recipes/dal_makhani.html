Dal Makhani

Discription

Dal Makhani or maa di dal, as it is popularly known in the Punjab, with its smooth velvety texture and lovely flavour is a delicacy that is very much a dish of the Punjab. Enjoy our Dal Makhani recipe with step by step photos and video.

Every Punjabi restaurant, roadside eating place and food stall vendor makes the claim that Punjabi Dal Makhani is a delicacy that they alone can make to perfection

Ingrediants

  • 3/4 cup whole urad (whole black lentil)
  • 2 tbsp rajma (kidney beans)
  • Salt to taste
  • 3 tbsp butter
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds (jeera)
  • 2 green chillies, cut lengthwise
  • 25 mm stick cinnamon (dalchini)
  • 2 cloves (laung / lavang)
  • 3 cardamom (elaichi)
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped onion
  • 1/2 tsp ginger-garlic (adrak-lehsun) paste
  • 1 tsp chilli powder
  • 1/4 tsp turmeric powder (haldi)
  • 1 1/2 cups fresh tomato pulp
  • 1/2 cup cream
  • 2 tbsp chopped coriander (dhania)
  • 1 tbsp fresh cream for garnish

Steps

  1. Clean, wash and soak the whole urad and rajma overnight.
  2. Drain, add 2 cups of water and salt, then pressure cook for 7 whistles or until the dals are overcooked.
  3. Allow the steam to escape before opening the lid.
  4. Whisk until the dal is almost mashed. Keep aside.
  5. For the tempering, heat the butter in a deep pan and add the cumin seeds.
  6. When the cumin seeds crackle, add the green chillies, cinnamon, cloves, cardamoms, and onions. Saut√© until the onions turn golden brown.
  7. Add the ginger-garlic paste, chilli powder, turmeric powder and tomato puree. Cook over medium flame until the mixture releases oil.
  8. Add the cooked dal, salt, and approx. 2 to 3 tbsp of water if needed. Simmer for 10 to 15 minutes.
  9. Add the cream and mix well. Simmer for another 2 to 3 minutes.
  10. Serve the dal makhani hot, garnished with coriander and fresh cream.
