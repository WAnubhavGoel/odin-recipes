Shahi Paneer

Discription

It's a dish that turns ordinary moments into extraordinary memories. This creamy delight isn't just a meal; it's a celebration on your plate, reserved for those special occasions that make family time truly magical. Try this delectable recipe at home.

The History of Shahi Paneer in India: Shahi Paneer Masala is a culinary marvel from the Mughal era that was born as a delightful mistake in royal kitchens. Legend has it that while preparing malai kofta, a chef accidentally dropped paneer cubes into the simmering gravy, leading to a magical discovery. This mishap inspired the creation of Shahi Paneer, a dish cherished for its rich history and exquisite taste. While the authenticity of this story remains shrouded in mystery, one undeniable truth prevails – Shahi Paneer continues to weave its magic in every delectable bite, offering a taste of royalty to all who savour it.

Ingrediants

  • Onion
  • Tamoto
  • Ginger
  • Garlic
  • Butter
  • Coriander Powder
  • Red Chilli Powder
  • Paneer
  • Water
  • Salt

Steps

  1. In a pan, heat 2 tbsp oil. Take spices and sauté on low flame.
  2. Add 1 inch ginger, 4 cloves garlic, and 2 onions. Sauté until the onions turn golden brown.
  3. Add 2 tomatoes, 2 tbsp badam and cook till the tomatoes soften.
  4. Cool completely, and transfer to the mixer jar.
  5. Add ¼ cup curd, and grind to a smooth paste.
  6. Sieve the puree to get a silky smooth texture. Keep aside.
  7. In a Kadai, take 2 tbsp butter and 2 tbsp oil. Add ½ tsp turmeric, 1 tsp chilli powder, 1 tsp coriander powder, and ½ tsp cumin powder.
  8. Sauté on low flame till the spices turn aromatic.
  9. Add the prepared puree and cook well.
  10. Add ¼ cup cream, 1 tsp salt, and cook till the oil separates.
  11. Add 20 cubes paneer, a few strands of kesar, 1 tsp sugar, and mix well.
  12. Simmer for 5 minutes, or until the flavours are well absorbed.
  13. Add 2 tbsp kasuri methi, 2 tbsp coriander, and mix well.
  14. Finally, enjoy Shahi Paneer with roti or naan.
